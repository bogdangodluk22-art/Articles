01 · THE PROBLEM

Auto retail in 2026 —
still just a site with photos

You found the car of your dreams. You clicked through to the dealer's website. You saw 8 photos from the same angles, a spec table, and a "Submit a request" button. You closed the tab.

This happens millions of times a day. Not because buyers don't want to buy. But because no website lets you feel the car through a screen.

Friday, 10:30 PM. You scroll through 12 car-dealer websites in a row. Everywhere the same photos on a white background, a table of liters and kilowatts, and a manager who will "call you back tomorrow." You want to feel the car — but through a screen that's impossible. In the end you close everything and go to sleep. The decision is still not made.

It's not a question of budget. And not a question of desire. It's a question of experience.

People buy cars for €50,000 — but they choose them in 30 seconds on a website. And if nothing hooked them in those 30 seconds — they've gone to a competitor.

73% of visitors to car websites leave the page without contacting the dealer. Not because of price — because of the absence of emotion and a real experience of interacting with the product.

The problem is systemic. A traditional online showroom is a PDF catalog moved onto the internet. No interactivity. No AI. No feeling that you're holding the wheel.

📸
2D photos instead of experience
8 shots from one angle won't replace a 360° view. The client doesn't feel the car — they look at it like a picture in a catalog.
📞
A manager who "will call back"
A question at 11:00 PM goes unanswered until morning. And by morning the client is already with a competitor. A 24/7 AI consultant isn't a luxury — it's a necessity.
🧮
Import cost — a separate quest
Excise tax, customs duty, VAT, shipping — the buyer has to calculate it themselves or call a broker. Most simply never make it to this step.

I decided to build what didn't exist in Ukraine. Not yet another site with cars. But a full-fledged interactive 3D showroom where the client feels the car before they ever drive to the dealership. With an AI consultant that answers instantly. With an import calculator right in the interface. With a collection of cars from four countries around the world.

05 · ARCHITECTURE

How it works
under the hood

The whole product is a single HTML file. Two independent Babylon.js engines (Hero + Garage), four AI tools, a multilingual interface and a Telegram bot. No frameworks. No dependencies. Maximum control over every line.

⚙ AUTOSMART · WORKFLOW ARCHITECTURE
Babylon.js
Two independent 3D engines — Hero and Garage. They stop via IntersectionObserver when their section isn't visible. GLB models with Draco compression, cached in memory.
GPT-4o via n8n
The INFO tool — a webhook POST to n8n, GPT-4o responds in 2–3 seconds. Temperature 0.7, max_tokens 400. No direct API keys in the frontend.
COMP · CALC · PICK — Frontend
Three tools run entirely on the frontend without a single API request. The CAR_SPECS object (20 cars × 5 parameters), the excise formula, the PICK_DATA scoring algorithm.
Telegram Bot @autosmart_3bot
A separate HTML page as a Telegram Web App. The AI consultant knows the current car via a URL parameter. user_id from Telegram.WebApp.initDataUnsafe.
Hostinger VPS · GitHub
nginx in Docker — serves the HTML and static assets. GLB models and photos on GitHub raw (critical files moved to the VPS to avoid rate-limits). n8n self-hosted on the same VPS.

11 · GLOSSARY OF TERMS
📖 EXPAND THE GLOSSARY OF TERMS
Babylon.js
An open-source JavaScript framework for 3D rendering in the browser. Uses WebGL. In AutoSmart — two independent engines for the Hero and Garage sections.
GLB
A binary 3D model format (Binary glTF). More compact than regular glTF. In AutoSmart — 20 GLB files with Draco compression, 2–5 MB each.
Draco
A 3D geometry compression algorithm from Google. Reduces the size of GLB files by 2–5× without noticeable quality loss.
n8n
An open-source workflow automation tool. An analog of Zapier, but self-hosted. In AutoSmart — it receives requests from the frontend and passes them to GPT-4o.
Webhook
A URL that receives HTTP POST requests from external services. In AutoSmart — the frontend sends a question to the n8n webhook and gets the AI's answer.
IntersectionObserver
A browser API for tracking element visibility in the viewport. In AutoSmart — it stops the 3D render loop when a section isn't visible, saving GPU.
Telegram Web App
An HTML page opened inside Telegram via a Mini App. It has access to user data through the Telegram.WebApp API. In AutoSmart — a separate AI consultant.
ACES Tone Mapping
Academy Color Encoding System — a color-processing standard from the film industry. In Babylon.js it gives 3D models a cinematic look with realistic contrast.
